    Hypothermia

    Hypothermia Alert

    We alert you when your loved one is out in unsafe cold conditions that could lead to hypothermia, whether they’re miles away or right on their own front porch.

    Heat Exhaustion

    Heat Alert

    We alert you if your loved one is at risk for heat exhaustion due to being out for too long on extremely hot days, no matter how close or far away they may be.

    Water Safety

    Water Alert

    We alert you to water dangers, whenever your loved one might be minutes away from dangerous waters, to avoid risk of accidental drowning.

    Traffic Safety

    Traffic Alert

    We monitor traffic patterns near your loved one, and alert you if they get too close to dangerous, busy, multi-lane roads or highways to avoid risk of traffic-related dangers and accidents.

    Falling

    Fall Alert

    We alert you if your loved one has fallen and requires help, no matter where they are.

    Group Hazard Curation

    Group Hazard Curation

    Caregivers’ network of friends & family act as eyes-on-the-ground, logging hazards in their local environment.

    Safety Zones

    Safety Zone

    Receive alerts when your loved one exits predetermined safety zones.

    Who Can Help

    Who Can Help

    Caregivers can see at a glance who in their network is nearby in case help is needed at any given moment.

    Group Rescue

    Group Rescue

    Caregivers can deploy local friends and family to the rescue when the caregiver is away from home.

    Floor Location

    Floor Location

    Caregivers and rescue workers can locate lost ones on specific floors in tall buildings.

    Windowless Environment

    Windowless Environment

    Finding lost ones in windowless rooms and basements is made possible with multi-cell-tower triangulation.

    Amazing Battery

    Amazing Battery

    Our battery lasts for weeks on a single charge, reducing the stress of needing constant recharges, and easily allowing the necessary minimum days recommended by National Search and Rescue, in case of emergency.

    Community Respite Care

    Community Respite Care

    Friends and family can help caregivers by taking on caregiving responsibilities or helping with basic but necessary tasks such as grocery shopping.
